Worksheet — Describing Change
Interpreting, Presenting & Questioning Business Charts
Handout — Page 3 of 4
A. Open Questions — Interpret the Charts

Study the four charts carefully. Answer each question in one or two complete sentences. Use language of change: direction, degree and speed. Be as precise as possible — include numbers where you can.

  1. 1. Look at Chart 1. How did total revenue change between 2020 and 2024? Was this change consistent every year? Explain what happened in 2023 and suggest a word to describe the overall trend.
  2. 2. Compare the operating profit in 2022 and 2023 (Chart 1). How significant was this change? Which words from the vocabulary list would you use to describe this fall — and why?
  3. 3. Look at Chart 2. Which department saw the largest percentage increase in costs between 2022 and 2024? Approximately how much did costs rise in this department?
  4. 4. Did any department manage to reduce its costs between 2022 and 2024? (Chart 2). Describe this change using an appropriate noun and adverb.
  5. 5. Compare the two pie charts (Chart 3). Which segment grew the most between 2022 and 2024? Which segment declined? Mention the specific percentage change for both.
  6. 6. Looking at Chart 3, what strategic trend can you identify in the company's business? What might explain this shift? Use at least one phrase from the vocabulary list.
  7. 7. Study Chart 4. What happened to the number of contractors between 2020 and 2024? How does this compare to the change in permanent staff? Use different vocabulary for each group.
  8. 8. Looking at all four charts together, what is your overall assessment of this company's performance over the five-year period? Give at least two pieces of evidence and link two charts in your answer.

C. Presentation Task — Instructions
1.Introduction (approx. 1 min): Introduce yourself and the topic. Explain what the four charts show, the period they cover, and what company the data is from.
2.Chart by chart (3–4 min): Present each chart in order. For each one: identify the main trend, highlight one key or surprising change, and use precise language. Include at least one specific figure (e.g. "Sales costs rose sharply by approximately 59%, from €1.1M to €1.75M").
3.Overall assessment (1 min): Summarise the company's performance. Mention one strength and one area of concern. Link data from at least two different charts in your conclusion.
4.Questions: Invite questions and respond using: "That's a good question — if you look at Chart…" or "The data suggests that…" or "According to the figures…"
Useful signposting phrases:   "Let me draw your attention to…"  |  "As you can see from Chart…"  |  "This is particularly striking because…"  |  "Moving on to the next chart…"  |  "To summarise the key points…"  |  "What is especially notable is…"

Grammar note — Noun or Verb?  You can express the same idea in two ways. Both are correct; the noun form is more formal and common in written reports:
  ▸ Verb + adverb: "Revenue rose sharply in 2022."
  ▸ There was + article + adjective + noun: "There was a sharp rise in revenue in 2022."
Model Sentences — Use These as a Guide
▸ Revenue rose steadily over the five-year period, from €18.2M in 2020 to €24.1M in 2024, despite a temporary dip in 2023.
▸ Operating profit fell sharply in 2023, reaching its lowest point at €2.1M — a dramatic drop from the 2022 peak of €4.6M.
▸ There was a considerable increase in R&D spending between 2022 and 2024, with costs rising substantially from €900K to €1.4M — an increase of approximately 55%.
▸ The Export segment grew gradually from 20% to 27% of total revenue, indicating a clear shift in the company's business strategy towards international markets.
▸ The number of contractors surged dramatically — more than tripling from 20 to 65 — while permanent headcount rose only slightly from 148 to 162 over the same period.
