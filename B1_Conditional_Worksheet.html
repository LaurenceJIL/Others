Grammar Reference

1st Conditional If + present simple, will + infinitive  →  "If the client signs today, we will offer a 5% discount."
2nd Conditional If + past simple, would + infinitive  →  "If we had a bigger budget, we would hire a sales team."

Discussion
Respond to the scenario using the correct conditional form
4 pts
Read each scenario carefully. Write your response using the correct conditional, then discuss with a partner. Q13–14: 1st conditional  |  Q15–16: 2nd conditional.
13
1st Conditional Realistic & possible situation
You are a sales representative. Your manager tells you that the company will offer a bonus if the whole team hits their monthly target. You are the only person who hasn't yet reached it.
Will you reach your target this month? What will you do differently in the final few days? Start your answer with "If I..."
14
1st Conditional Realistic & possible situation
You are an account manager at a software company. A long-term client has said they will only renew their contract if you can guarantee a 15% reduction in their annual fee.
What will you say to your line manager? What will happen if the client doesn't renew? Use at least one 1st conditional sentence.
15
2nd Conditional Hypothetical situation
Imagine you were head of sales for your company — not your current role, but a hypothetical senior position with full authority over strategy, team structure and budget.
What would you change? What would the impact be? Start with "If I were head of sales, I would..."
16
2nd Conditional Hypothetical situation
Your company currently has no international presence. Imagine, hypothetically, that your company had the full resources and personnel to expand into any global market.
Which market would you enter and why? What challenges would you face? What would success look like?
