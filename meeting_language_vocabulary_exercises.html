Meeting Language

Vocabulary exercises for B1/B2 professional English learners

Agreeing

  • That's exactly my thinking.
  • I couldn't agree more.
  • Absolutely — that's a solid point.
  • You're right, and I'd add that…
  • That aligns perfectly with our goals.

Partially agreeing

  • I see where you're coming from, although…
  • That's a fair point, but there's another side.
  • I agree up to a point, however…
  • You make a valid case, though I'd qualify that.
  • Largely yes, but I'd nuance it slightly.

Partial disagreement

  • I take your point, but I'm not fully convinced.
  • That may be true in theory, but in practice…
  • I'm not entirely sure I'd go that far.
  • There's merit there, yet the data suggests otherwise.

Polite disagreement

  • I understand your perspective, but I see it differently.
  • With respect, I'd have to disagree on that.
  • I appreciate your view; mine is somewhat different.
  • I'm afraid I can't quite go along with that.

Strong disagreement

  • I have to strongly disagree — the evidence shows…
  • That's fundamentally at odds with what we know.
  • I really can't accept that position.
  • With all due respect, that simply doesn't hold up.

Asking for clarification

  • Could you elaborate on what you mean by that?
  • Would you mind clarifying that last point?
  • Could you walk us through the reasoning?
  • Just to be clear — are you saying that…?
  • What exactly do you mean in practical terms?

Suggesting an alternative

  • What if we approached it from a different angle?
  • Could we consider another option here?
  • Have we thought about doing it this way instead?
  • I'd like to propose a different course of action.
